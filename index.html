About

The purpose of the term project is to follow key phases of software product development. It is a chance to pilot, adopt and implement the best practices covered in the course.

Refrigerator Raider is a web-based application designed for both personal and commercial refrigerator inventory management. The app allows users to input products and related quantities into an online virtual refrigerator and receive alerts when items are running low.

Help

Refrigerator Raider is a web-based application designed for both personal and commercial refrigerator inventory management. The app allows users to input products and related quantities into an online Virtual refrigerator and receive alerts when items are running low. For help or enhancement requests, please contact us at (818) 555-5111 or at customerservice@abcmsdevelopment.com.

Q: Who are the creators?

A: The creators of this project are all students at Boston University. The project team was established as a part of a Boston University Metropolitan College class, MET CS633, Software Quality, Testing and Security Management.

Q: Where did the idea of Refrigerator Raiders come from?

A: The team originally started out with a bunch of different ideas, including a meal tracking application to input and view nutritional content and an application that allows you to share what food is in your fridge and it provides recipes based on that information. With further team discussion, the idea evolved into Refrigerator Raiders.

Q: What did you use to create the application?

To create the application, we used HTML5/JavaScript/PHP and other JavaScript Libraries and HTML Templates.

Q: How come I cannot view my friend or relative's fridge?

A: The fridge owner must grant a Refrigerator raider (or view only user) to see the contents of the fridge. If you have trouble with setting up these permissions, please contact us at (818) 555-5111 or at customerservice@abcmsdevelopment.com.

Q: What enhancements will come next for Refrigerator Raiders?

A: With the short class deadline, we limited our scope for the first release. Some enhancement ideas include: identifying sale items, allowing actual ordering to supermarkets and API sites like Amazon Fresh, filtering options, sending emails for list capabilities, pictures of each item, scanning item barcode to input it into system, and more.
